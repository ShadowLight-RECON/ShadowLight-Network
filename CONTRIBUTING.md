# Contributing to ShadowLight Network

Thank you for your interest in contributing to ShadowLight Network.

ShadowLight is a precision‑driven analytical framework. Contributions are welcome, but they must align with the project’s philosophy, scope, and standards.

This document explains how to contribute effectively.

---

## Project Philosophy

ShadowLight Network prioritizes:

- Conceptual clarity over feature volume
- Structured reasoning over speculation
- Intentional design over rapid iteration
- Documentation‑first development

If your contribution strengthens these principles, it is likely welcome.

---

## What You Can Contribute

Meaningful contributions include:

- Improvements to documentation clarity or structure
- Refinements to architectural descriptions
- Thoughtful additions to modes or analytical behavior
- Corrections, consistency fixes, or precision enhancements
- Well‑reasoned proposals aligned with ShadowLight’s scope

Low‑effort or speculative contributions will not be accepted.

---

## Contribution Guidelines

Before contributing, ensure that your work:

- Aligns with the existing philosophy and design intent
- Is clearly written and well‑structured
- Avoids introducing unnecessary complexity
- Does not expand scope without justification
- Respects existing terminology and concepts

All contributions should be intentional and explain *why* they exist, not just *what* they add.

---

## How to Contribute

1. **Fork the repository**
2. **Create a focused branch**
